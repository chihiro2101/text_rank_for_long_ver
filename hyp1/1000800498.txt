one inscribed 'yehohanan ben hagkol ' that contained an iron nail in a heel bone suggesting crucifixion ; another inscribed 'james son of joseph , brother of jesus ' , the authenticity of which is not supported by most scholars ; and ten ossuaries recovered from the talpiot tomb in 1980 , several of which are reported to have names recorded in the new testament the custom of secondary burial in ossuaries , on a whole , did not persist among jews past the second temple period nor appear to exist widely among jews outside the land of israel there are , of course , exceptions to every trend : after the destruction of the second temple , poor imitations of ossuaries made of clay were created in galilee ;
